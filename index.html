Our Programs/Projects

Me Boafo

Members receive working capital support in times of emergency.

members receive working capital support in times of emergency.

So Mi Mu

This is where a member decides to become a sponsor or supplier to a member project for returns. A member can also engage the organisation to manage or revive its business or asset on their behalf

Nkabom

This is where members contribute towards social development projects for returns payable annually in proportion to contribution paid annually.

Fa Kakra Sie

This is where members contribute on behalf of their minors or dependents to obtain support when they attain the age of 18years

Fa Begye

This is where members bring raw materials for monetary returns or Benefits in Kind.

Profile of CEO

Abigail Ofosu

CA, MSc, BSc MSMEs Development Specialist

Abigail Ofosu is a Chartered Accountant with special focus on MSMEs development, Women and Youth development, Financial Literacy and Child Right Advocacy. An Entrepreneur and a Trainer specializing in Trade & Business, Consulting with the Business Resource Centre – Shama under the Ministry of Trade and Industry, Rural Enterprises Programme and the Ghana Enterprises Agency. Bringing more than 15 years of experience, She has been involved in the design of Financial Service Support and Risk Management at Akuapem Rural Bank and Wapic Insurance Ghana now Coronation Insurance. Miss Ofosu served as a coordinator for Makola Foundation a non- governmental organisation for the informal market traders and Project Manager for Street of Gold foundation a Child right organisation. She has volunteered in several capacities to promote the development of communities by designing policies and interventions to support the vulnerable.Abigail Ofosu holds Masters of Science Degree in International Accounting and Finance from the Buckinghamshire New University UK as well as a Bachelor of Science Degree in Business Administration-Accounting Option from School of Business-KNUST. She is a Member of the Institute of Chartered Accountants Ghana, Institute of Internal Auditors Ghana, Student member of the Chartered Institute of Tax Accountants Ghana and a Coordinator for Women In MICE-Ghana. She is fluent in English, Akuapem, Twi, Ga and a French Beginner.

Country Care Network (CCN)

Country Care Network (CCN) is a social enterprise registered to promote economic and social development by providing emergency working capital support, entrepreneurial Training to Individuals, Unemployed, Startups and Existing Business. CCN provides a linkage for resource providers and resource users.

The platform seeks to provide marketing support for Products and Services, As well as the development of Value Chains and Supply Chains.

We design Projects, Programs and Interventions to raise finance and support from Partners and Sponsors to support Entrepreneurship, Innovations and Industrialisation. We also ensure that there is access to information and basic services in relation to Financing, Agriculture, Education, Health, Technology, Energy, Water, and Sanitation.
